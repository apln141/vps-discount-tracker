## What Makes a VPS Discount Actually "Good"?

A cheap VPS isn't automatically a good deal. I've seen $1/month servers that can barely run `apt update` without choking. A real VPS discount means you're paying less for something that would already be worth full price.

Here's what separates a genuine discount from a trap:

- **Hardware quality matters more than price.** A $15/year plan on AMD EPYC with NVMe storage is a bargain. A $10/year plan on a 10-year-old Xeon with spinning disks? That's just someone renting you e-waste.
- **The discount should be sustainable.** A one-month promo that reverts to triple the price is not a deal — it's a bait-and-switch dressed up in a coupon code.
- **Network routing is the silent value killer.** You can have the fastest server on earth, but if the route to your users zig-zags through congested peering points, your "discount" VPS becomes an expensive frustration.
- **Recurring discounts beat one-time promos.** A 50% lifetime discount on a $36/year plan saves you $18 every single year. A 50% first-month-only discount on a $36/month plan saves you... $18 once. Do the math.

## VPS Discount Traps: What to Watch For

Not every discount deserves your click. Here are three red flags I've learned to spot:

**1. The "Too Cheap to Be Real" Test**

If a $3/year VPS promises 4 cores, 8GB RAM, and "unlimited" bandwidth — it's probably oversold to the point of being unusable. Real hardware costs real money. The sweet spot for a genuinely good budget VPS discount lands somewhere between $12-$40/year for a usable 1-2 core instance. Below that, you're gambling.

**2. The Vanishing Discount**

Some providers advertise a discount but bury the fact that it only applies to the first billing cycle. Month two? Full price, baby. Always check whether a coupon code is recurring or introductory. ZgoCloud's **8NU44CM6LZ** is explicitly a lifetime recurring discount — that's the gold standard.

**3. The "No Refund on Discount Plans" Clause**

This one is worth reading. ZgoCloud's promo plans (the ones marked "Special Offer") come with no refunds. That's fairly standard in the budget VPS world — the margins are thin enough that processing refunds on a $15/year plan doesn't make business sense. The trade-off is that you get hardware at prices that shouldn't be possible. If you're comfortable with that arrangement, these plans represent some of the best VPS discount value anywhere.
